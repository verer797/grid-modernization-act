# Grid Modernization and Energy Affordability Act

## Preamble
This Act aims to modernize the national electricity grid, promote renewable energy sources, reduce energy bills for households and businesses, and create green jobs.

## Title I: Grid Infrastructure Modernization
### Section 101: Federal Grants for Grid Upgrades
The Department of Energy shall establish a program to provide grants to utilities for upgrading transmission and distribution infrastructure.

### Section 102: Smart Grid Deployment
Incentives for deployment of smart grid technologies that improve reliability and enable integration of distributed energy resources.

## Title II: Renewable Energy Promotion
### Section 201: Renewable Portfolio Standards
Utilities shall be required to source a minimum percentage of their electricity from renewable sources by 2030.

### Section 202: Tax Credits for Renewable Generation
Investment tax credits for solar, wind, and other qualifying renewable energy facilities.

### Section 203: Grants for Community Solar Projects
Funding for community‑owned solar installations that serve low‑ and moderate‑income households.

## Title III: Consumer Protection and Affordability
### Section 301: Energy Efficiency Rebates
Rebates for households that install energy‑efficient appliances, insulation, and windows.

### Section 302: Low‑Income Energy Assistance
Expansion of existing programs to help low‑income families pay energy bills.

## Title IV: Workforce Development
### Section 401: Green Jobs Training
Grants for vocational training programs focused on renewable energy installation, grid maintenance, and energy auditing.

## Title V: Implementation and Reporting
### Section 501: Reporting Requirements
Annual report to Congress on progress toward grid modernization and renewable energy targets.
